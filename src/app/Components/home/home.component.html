Active Park Assist

(APA)

Project team 3

Project Background

Cars have cameras and sensors for a variety of safety features. One additional way these can be used is with automatic parking assist. Pulling in to a parking spot correctly can be stressful, especially for parallel parking, and this feature can take that stress away from the driver.

Project Description

The Active Park Assist system is a feature that allows a vehicle to automatically park itself in either a parallel or perpendicular parking space and mitigate any risks that are associate with human-controlled parking.

The Active Park Assist will be integrated with the existing Ford vehicles and customers will also be able to transfer control of the vehicle to the Ford Pass app on their cell.

Since driver-controlled parking regularly leads to property damage due to a driver's inability to determine obstacles that may be in the paths of their vehicle, by installing depth-perception cameras onto the vehicle and integrating functionality that supports the steering, accelerating, and breaking functionalities of the vehicle, the ability to park is streamlined for a driver.

The Active Park Assist system will be able to provide the ability for a user to automatically park upon activation of the feature.
